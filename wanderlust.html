Wanderlust App

UX/UI Design

Wanderlust is a mobile app that allows users to explore travel inspiration by location. We focused our efforts on the photo and location sharing aspect of the app, which includes post submission and search functionality. Our objective was to curate user-generated content and organize posts in a way in which obscure locations would not be overshadowed by popular travel destinations.

Exploring Wanderlust

Wanderlust was intended to have two key features:

  • A photo-sharing system in which users can search and sort through user generated content
  • Local recommendations and reviews, which would allow users to learn about fun and inexpensive places in the area.

My teammate and I decided to explore the photo-sharing aspect of the app.

Competitive Analysis

We explored apps that involved travel, like Travelog, Yonder, and TripMayor. We also studied popular networks in which young adults typically post about their travels like Facebook, Instagram, and Tumblr. We analyzed our competitors based on their photo feeds, tagging and sorting systems, post submission, and social media.

Synthesis

An important feature we wanted to implement was the ability to post multiple photos in one post; apps like Instagram, Yonder, and TripMayor only allowed one photo posts which we thought limited the potential and depth of the photo-sharing experience.

Another aspect we found was that most travel apps functioned as social media outlets, with liking and commenting being an integral feature. Although many users were already familiar with the concept of liking and commenting, we decided that this feature deviated from our main objective, which was to present users with posts based on location, not by trending popularity. With that, we decided to use Tacking, a feature that is similar to tacking destinations to a world map, as a replacement for the liking system and a method in which users could compile a list of future destinations they are interested in visiting.

While interviewing users, a common issue that arose was the lack of quality posts of popular photo-sharing apps. To address this concern, we found that our users preferred curated content, which ensured that only quality travel posts reached the front page. This also addressed the concern we had with obscure locations being overshadowed by popular travel destinations.
